Research Summary

My background and experience are on advanced control system designs, with applications in mechatronic systems. The study relies on the following themes:

  • Iterative learning control
  • Feedback and feedforward control
  • Robust control
  • Optimization

After more than 5 years studying in the field of automatic control, I have gained a broad knowledge in many aspects of control theory (linear and nonlinear control, robust control, optimal control...), control design techniques (PID, loop-shaping, mu-synthesis, MIMO control....), and numerical optimization approaches (convex optimization, LMI,...).

As a control engineer, I am very skilled with MATLAB as it is the most ubiquitous software for matrix calculations and algebra. I have also gained experience with real-time implementation (dSPACE, Simulink) of control implementation and system identification.

My PhD research mainly focus on iterative learning control, a control method that improves tracking performance of repetitive systems (automation, manufacturing, batch process,...). The research topics are the following:

Research Topics

  • Iterative learning control (ILC)

    learning through experience and getting better...

    Iterative learning control (ILC) is widely used in control applications to improve performance of repetitive processes. Examples of ILC applications are: industrial robots (i.e. pick-and-place robots, gantry robots, cutting robots), motion systems (wafer stage, medical rehabilitation equipmentâ€¦), batch processes (chemical processes, rapid thermal processing). The key idea of ILC is to update the control signal iteratively based on measured data from previous trials, such that the output converges to the given reference trajectory.

  • Robust convergent norm-optimal ILC

    with overhead crane application

    In norm-optimal ILC, the input signal for the next trial is generated by solving an optimization problem. This approach can deal with input constraints efficiently. The cost function of the optimization problem relies on the system model, and hence model uncertainty can cause divergence and/or degrade the performance of norm-optimal ILC. To deal with this problem, we propose a robust worst-case norm-optimal ILC design, where model uncertainty is taken into account. The design problem is reformulated as a convex optimization problem, and can be solved efficiently.
